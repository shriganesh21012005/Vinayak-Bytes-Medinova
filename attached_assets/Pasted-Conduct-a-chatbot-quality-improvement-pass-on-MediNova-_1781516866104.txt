Conduct a chatbot quality improvement pass on MediNova.

Current issue:
The chatbot handles emergencies and health-memory questions reasonably well, but many normal health questions fall back to generic responses such as:

"Could you share a bit more detail about what you'd like to know?"

or unrelated templates (e.g. mental health advice for sleepiness).

Examples currently performing poorly:
- "Should I dance?"
- "Can I go outside in pollution without a mask?"
- "I'm feeling sleepy"
- "How much water should I drink?"
- "I have a mild headache"
- "Can I exercise today?"
- "What foods should I avoid?"
- "Is walking good for me?"

Goal:
Review the most common 50 health questions users are likely to ask.

Requirements:

1. Reduce unnecessary fallback responses.
2. Provide useful general-health guidance when safe.
3. Continue using HealthMemory where relevant.
4. Preserve all existing safety protections:
   - No diagnosis
   - No prescriptions
   - No replacing a doctor
   - Emergency detection must remain unchanged
5. Ensure responses are context-aware:
   - If user asks about allergies, medications, conditions, restrictions, use HealthMemory.
   - If user asks a general wellness question, answer it directly instead of requesting more details.
6. Remove cases where unrelated templates are returned.
   Example:
   - Sleepiness should not trigger mental-health advice.
   - Pollution questions should not trigger generic clarification requests.
7. Add automated test cases covering:
   - allergies
   - medications
   - sleep
   - exercise
   - diet
   - hydration
   - pollution
   - fever
   - headache
   - dizziness
   - emergency symptoms
   - diagnosis requests
   - prescription requests

Before making changes:
- Trace the exact response path.
- Identify where generic fallback responses are being selected.
- Explain the root causes.

After changes:
- Show what files were modified.
- Show example responses before vs after.
